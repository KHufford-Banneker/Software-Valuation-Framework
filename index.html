Software Valuation Framework

Enterprise software valuations are driven by the Rule of 40 (ARR Growth + EBITDA Margin), then adjusted for company scale and customer retention. In the current market, the sharpest predictor of valuation is sustainable growth paired with strong customer retention. Buyers are looking for companies that grow efficiently, retain and expand their customer base.

All adjustments use continuous linear interpolation between anchor points — every $1M of ARR and every 1% of retention produces an incremental impact. No cliff effects at band boundaries. Retention curves are asymmetric: penalties below baseline are steeper than premiums above. Formula: Final Multiple = Base Multiple × (1 – Size Discount) × (1 + NRR Adj. + GRR Adj.)

GRR is the foundation metric. Low GRR signals structural churn that expansion revenue cannot fix. A company with 120% NRR but 80% GRR gets zero net retention benefit — the GRR penalty fully offsets the NRR premium. You cannot outrun bad gross retention.

100% NRR is not good enough — it means zero expansion from existing customers, which carries a –15% penalty. The baseline is 105%, reflecting the minimum expansion buyers expect from a healthy SaaS business. Even strong NRR cannot compensate for poor GRR — high expansion built on a churning base is unsustainable.
